# Replicable Operations Library

## Purpose

This folder will hold the approved operating methods that allow trained people and locations to deliver the same safe, high-quality service.

## Required Manual Set

- Client inquiry, fit, and scope screening
- Emergency and safeguarding response
- Agreement, privacy notice, consent, and authorization
- Structured intake
- Action planning and client approval
- Provider research and credential verification
- Appointment and logistics coordination
- Client and authorized-family communication
- Expense and scope-change approval
- Complaints, incidents, near misses, and privacy events
- Client access and correction requests
- Closeout, retention, return, and destruction
- Quality review and corrective action
- Software and information handling
- Location setup and opening
- Business continuity and founder absence

## Location Operations File

Each location should maintain:

- Approved service area and packages
- Responsible manager and privacy/safety contacts
- Local licences and insurance evidence
- Staff roles, access, training, and competency
- Verified local provider directory
- Referral and community-development plan
- Local capacity, budget, and scorecard
- Emergency and business-continuity contacts
- Complaints, incidents, and corrective actions
- Opening approval and ongoing review evidence

## Standardization Rule

Every instruction must be labelled as:

- Mandatory standard
- Approved method with professional judgment
- Permitted local adaptation
- Founder or specialist escalation
- Prohibited activity

Mandatory privacy, consent, safety, non-clinical, incident, brand-claim, and credential-verification standards may not be weakened by a location or individual operator.

## Proof of Replicability

A procedure is not considered transferable until:

1. It is documented.
2. A second person is trained.
3. That person performs it in a fictional or controlled setting.
4. Performance is reviewed against written criteria.
5. Findings are corrected.
6. The person performs it successfully during normal operations.
7. The procedure remains effective without informal founder intervention.
