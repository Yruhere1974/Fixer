# Founder Independence and Team Plan

## Objective

Increase service capacity and business value while preserving the founder's distinctive judgment and client trust. The goal is not to remove the founder from the business immediately; it is to ensure that the founder participates by choice and strategic value rather than because routine operations cannot continue without her.

## Work Classification

Classify each activity.

### Founder Signature Work

Work where the founder's personal involvement is currently part of the value proposition. Document why, price it appropriately, and avoid pretending it is scalable until the role changes.

### Trainable Navigator Work

Client-facing coordination that can be taught through standards, simulations, observation, supervision, and review.

### Administrative Work

Scheduling, approved communications, data entry, reminders, document assembly, directory maintenance, invoicing support, and reporting that should not consume founder capacity once properly controlled.

### Specialist Work

Legal, privacy, accounting, insurance, clinical, safeguarding, technology, or intellectual-property work that requires qualified outside expertise.

### Prohibited Work

Clinical assessment, treatment, diagnosis, medication advice, undisclosed conflicts, unauthorized information sharing, and other activities outside the approved scope.

## Knowledge-Capture Method

For each repeated founder activity:

1. Observe a real or fictional example.
2. Record the purpose and desired result.
3. Identify the information the founder examines.
4. Identify questions asked and signals noticed.
5. Separate routine decisions from judgment-heavy decisions.
6. Write the standard, examples, and escalation points.
7. Test the instructions with another person.
8. Record misunderstandings and revise.
9. Observe supervised performance.
10. Approve independent performance only after verification.

Do not place identifiable client information in training materials unless there is a professionally approved basis. Prefer fictional or properly de-identified cases.

## Role Architecture

### Founder and Strategic Lead

Accountable for vision, brand, major relationships, high-risk escalation, system improvement, leadership, capital allocation, and expansion gates.

### Lead Navigator

Owns client engagements within approved scope, reviews action plans, supervises navigators, monitors quality, and escalates exceptions.

### Navigator

Conducts approved intake, research, planning, coordination, communication, follow-up, and closeout after demonstrated competency.

### Client Coordinator

Handles controlled scheduling, reminders, document assembly, status tracking, and approved communications without making clinical or high-risk decisions.

### Provider-Network Coordinator

Maintains provider categories, sources, verification dates, conflicts, accessibility information, and stale-entry reviews.

### Operations and Privacy Lead

Maintains procedures, permissions, complaints, incidents, information requests, retention, access reviews, training records, and evidence.

### Location Manager

Owns staffing, capacity, local referral development, quality, budget, incidents, compliance, and reporting for an approved location.

One person may hold several roles initially, but responsibilities and access must remain explicit.

## Competency Path

Every client-facing role should progress through:

1. Scope and policy learning
2. Privacy, consent, safety, accessibility, and boundary training
3. Fictional-case simulation
4. Observation
5. Supervised task performance
6. Supervised client engagement
7. Formal competency review
8. Limited independent authority
9. Periodic observation and reapproval
10. Additional training after changes, incidents, or quality failures

Training completion is not proof of competence. Approval requires observed performance against a standard.

## Founder-Absence Tests

Run progressively:

- One business day
- Three business days
- One week
- Two weeks
- Thirty days

Before each test, identify which responsibilities will transfer. During the test, record decisions, delays, escalations, client effects, revenue effects, and founder interventions.

The test fails when the founder quietly continues performing work behind the scenes. Every intervention must be logged as a remaining dependency.

## Team Scorecard

- Percentage of service hours delivered without founder involvement
- Founder review time by engagement
- Tasks returned for correction
- Escalations made correctly and missed
- Client experience by team member
- Privacy, safety, and quality events
- Training and competency status
- Employee retention
- Revenue and contribution per delivery role
- Capacity available and wait-list size
- Procedures successfully performed by at least two people

## Quarterly Goal

Reduce one material founder dependency each quarter without weakening client safety, privacy, quality, or the brand.
