Personal information is not a free-standing asset that may automatically be transferred or exploited. Any access or transfer during financing, franchising, due diligence, or sale requires privacy and legal review, minimum disclosure, appropriate authority, safeguards, and documented handling.

## Ownership Questions

For every material asset, verify:

- Was it created by the founder personally or for the company?
- Has ownership been assigned to the correct company where needed?
- Did an employee, contractor, designer, photographer, consultant, or software provider create part of it?
- Do the agreements address ownership and permitted use?
- Does it contain third-party material that the company only licenses?
- Does it contain client information or confidential provider information?
- Can it be transferred to a buyer or used by another location?
- Does a change-of-control clause require consent?
- Is renewal required?
- Can the company access it without the founder's personal account or device?

## Brand Independence Plan

Decide deliberately how the founder appears in the brand:

- Founder name as the master brand
- Independent company brand “founded by” the founder
- Company brand with the founder as spokesperson
- Company brand with no permanent founder connection

An independent company brand is generally easier to transfer, but the business may still use the founder's story and credibility under documented brand rules.

Build evidence that clients trust:

- The company promise
- Consistent service standards
- Team credentials and training
- Clear privacy and ethics commitments
- Reliable systems
- Approved local relationships

## Intellectual-Property Actions

- [ ] Select the long-term business and service names.
- [ ] Search for conflicting business names, domains, and trademarks with qualified assistance.
- [ ] Obtain advice on trademark strategy.
- [ ] Register and protect domains and key account names through business-controlled accounts.
- [ ] Use written agreements for commissioned brand, website, photography, writing, training, and software work.
- [ ] Confirm company rights to employee and contractor work product.
- [ ] Mark confidential manuals and control access.
- [ ] Maintain version, approval, and distribution records.
- [ ] Establish rules for franchisee, licensee, partner, and employee use.
- [ ] Monitor misuse and follow professional advice on enforcement.

The Canadian Intellectual Property Office describes trademarks as potentially valuable business assets and provides official guidance on registration and use. Trademark registration and enforcement strategy should be developed with qualified advice.

- Confidential access reviewed

- Brand use consistent
