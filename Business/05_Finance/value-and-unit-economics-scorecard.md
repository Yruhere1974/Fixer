# Value and Unit-Economics Scorecard

## Purpose

Prove that the business produces sustainable value after accounting for all labour, central support, risk controls, and location costs. Revenue alone does not establish scalability or sale value.

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|

Record unpaid founder time at a reasonable replacement cost. A service is not proven profitable if it works only because the founder does substantial unrecorded work.

## Client Acquisition

Track:

- Inquiries by source
- Acquisition spending by source
- Founder networking time
- Referral-partner development time
- Conversion rate
- Acquisition cost
- Time to conversion
- Revenue and contribution by source
- Repeat and referral behaviour

This reveals whether growth is transferable or depends on the founder's personal relationships.

## Team Economics

- Revenue supported per role
- Paid hours versus available hours
- Client delivery versus administration, training, and rework
- Supervision required
- Founder review time
- Training cost and time to competence
- Turnover and replacement cost
- Quality and incident cost

## Location-Level Economics

Before approving a second location, model and then measure:

- Local market size and target clients
- Expected inquiries and conversion
- Service mix
- Local pricing
- Local staffing and management
- Travel and service radius
- Marketing and referral development
- Licences, insurance, professional fees, and premises
- Software and central-service allocation
- Training and launch support
- Working capital
- Break-even client volume and date
- Cash required before self-sufficiency
- Location contribution after central costs

## Franchisor Economics

If franchising is considered, separately model:

- Franchise development and professional costs
- Disclosure and agreement maintenance
- Recruitment and qualification of prospects
- Training and launch support
- Ongoing operations, marketing, quality, privacy, and incident support
- Field reviews
- Software and reporting support
- Brand protection and enforcement
- Dispute and closure costs
- Revenue from initial fees, continuing fees, and approved services
- Number of locations required to support the central organization

Initial fees should not be treated as a substitute for a sustainable continuing support model.

## Owner-Adjusted Financial Review

For buyer and management purposes, distinguish:

- Salary for work performed
- Owner distributions
- Personal or discretionary expenses
- One-time startup expenses
- One-time legal, brand, or software investments
- Market-rate replacement cost for founder roles
- Recurring operating expenses

Any adjustment used to present business value must be supportable and not misleading.

## Value Quality Indicators

A stronger business generally demonstrates:

- Clean, timely financial records
- Consistent revenue and contribution
- Growing recurring revenue
- Low client and referral concentration
- Market-rate labour included in margins
- Limited founder dependence
- Stable team and low rework
- Documented systems and owned assets
- Predictable cash needs
- No hidden legal, tax, privacy, employment, or insurance liabilities

## Review Rhythm

- Weekly: cash, overdue receivables, capacity, and exceptions
- Monthly: complete business and service scorecards
- Quarterly: pricing, staffing, founder dependence, and forecast
- Annually: normalized financial review and valuation-readiness assessment with qualified advisors
