# Expansion and Franchise Readiness Gates

## Purpose

Prevent expansion from getting ahead of evidence, management capacity, client protection, or legal readiness.

## Growth Options to Compare

At each expansion decision, compare:

- Add capacity in the existing location
- Hire navigators
- Add a local manager
- Open a company-owned branch
- Serve a nearby area from the current operation
- Enter a strategic partnership
- License limited intellectual property
- Franchise the complete business system
- Remain focused and maximize profitability

The chosen label does not determine whether franchise law applies. Obtain specialist advice before offering any arrangement involving payment, brand use, and significant operational control or assistance.

## Gate 1: Core Business Proof

- [ ] Target client and problem are clear.
- [ ] Service packages are stable.
- [ ] Clients pay the intended prices.
- [ ] Time and direct cost are measured.
- [ ] Client workflow is complete.
- [ ] Privacy, consent, safety, and incident controls are proven.
- [ ] Demand is not based on a temporary promotion or one referral source.
- [ ] Complaints and failures have been incorporated into the system.
- [ ] Financial records are current and reliable.

**Decision:** Continue proving, proceed to team delivery, or stop/redesign.

## Gate 2: Team Delivery Proof

- [ ] Role descriptions and authority are clear.
- [ ] Training and competency checks are complete.
- [ ] A non-founder performs defined work consistently.
- [ ] Founder review time is measured.
- [ ] Escalations occur correctly.
- [ ] Client experience meets the standard.
- [ ] Market-rate labour still produces acceptable contribution.
- [ ] Individual accounts and information access are controlled.
- [ ] The company can manage a planned founder absence.

**Decision:** Strengthen team delivery or proceed to location testing.

## Gate 3: Second-Market or Location Pilot

- [ ] Market and community needs are validated.
- [ ] Local provider network is verified.
- [ ] Local business licensing and other requirements are confirmed.
- [ ] Location manager or accountable leader is ready.
- [ ] Budget and working capital are approved.
- [ ] Central support capacity is available.
- [ ] Local referral plan is ready.
- [ ] Privacy, incident, complaint, and continuity responsibilities are clear.
- [ ] Location measures and stop criteria are approved.
- [ ] Brand and service standards can be monitored.

**Decision:** Close, revise, continue, or approve the location as a proven model.

## Gate 4: Replication Evidence

- [ ] At least two operators have delivered the method successfully.
- [ ] The business has operated beyond the founder's direct oversight.
- [ ] The second market/location has produced adequate demand and quality evidence.
- [ ] Central-support effort is measured.
- [ ] Manuals reflect real operating differences.
- [ ] Location economics include all labour and central costs.
- [ ] The founder can explain which standards are mandatory and which may adapt locally.
- [ ] Brand ownership and use rules are documented.
- [ ] Buyer-style due diligence shows no unresolved critical gaps.

**Decision:** Continue company-owned growth, consider licensing, prepare for franchising, or remain focused.

## Gate 5: Franchise Feasibility

- [ ] Specialist franchise counsel confirms the proposed model and obligations.
- [ ] Corporate and tax structure is reviewed.
- [ ] Trademark and intellectual-property strategy is ready.
- [ ] Franchisor and franchisee unit economics are realistic.
- [ ] The company can fund and staff franchise support.
- [ ] Territory and site/market selection methods are documented.
- [ ] Franchisee profile and qualification standards are defined.
- [ ] Training, launch, ongoing support, field review, software, reporting, and marketing systems are ready.
- [ ] Privacy roles and client-information boundaries are clear among franchisor and franchisee.
- [ ] Insurance requirements are defined.
- [ ] Complaint, incident, crisis, closure, transfer, and default procedures are ready.
- [ ] Financial performance representations, if any, have specialist review and evidence.
- [ ] Disclosure-document inputs and supporting records are complete.
- [ ] The franchise prospect process is controlled.

**Decision:** Authorize legal franchise development or do not proceed.

## Gate 6: Franchise Offer Readiness

Before any prospective franchisee signs or pays:

- [ ] Current disclosure document is completed and certified as required.
- [ ] All material facts are included and current.
- [ ] Financial statements and required lists are current.
- [ ] All proposed agreements are included.
- [ ] Material-change process is active.
- [ ] Delivery date and method are recorded.
- [ ] Required waiting period is tracked.
- [ ] No unauthorized earnings or outcome claim is made.
- [ ] Prospect has been encouraged to obtain independent legal and financial advice.
- [ ] Approval is documented by franchise counsel and authorized company leadership.

B.C.'s Franchises Act generally requires the prospective franchisee to receive the required disclosure document at least 14 days before the earlier of signing a franchise-related agreement or paying relevant consideration. The document must be delivered as one complete document and include prescribed content and material facts. Specialist franchise counsel must control this process.

## Gate 7: New Operator or Location Launch

- [ ] Agreement and required disclosure process are complete.
- [ ] Operator funding and working capital are verified as agreed.
- [ ] Training and competency checks are passed.
- [ ] Local licensing, insurance, privacy, employment, and operating needs are complete.
- [ ] Provider network meets the minimum standard.
- [ ] Software, permissions, reporting, and support are operational.
- [ ] Marketing materials are approved.
- [ ] Incident and emergency exercises are passed.
- [ ] Opening review is signed.

## Gate 8: Ongoing Network Health

Review at least quarterly:

- Location quality and client experience
- Complaints, incidents, privacy events, and near misses
- Financial and cash health
- Staff competency and capacity
- Provider verification
- Brand compliance
- Marketing claims
- Support requests and unresolved issues
- Transfers, closures, renewals, or disputes
- System-wide improvements

## Franchise Evidence File

Maintain:

- Business and management background
- Corporate structure and affiliates
- Intellectual-property ownership
- Financial statements
- Initial and ongoing fee history
- Estimated investment evidence
- Operating cost evidence
- Current and former locations/operators
- Openings, transfers, closures, non-renewals, and terminations
- Litigation, insolvency, and material-event records
- Supplier arrangements and rebates
- Training and support descriptions
- Territory and competition policies
- All agreements
- Disclosure versions, certificates, delivery evidence, and material changes
