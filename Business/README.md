# Business Build and Scale Folder

## Purpose

This folder organizes the Kelowna Health & Wellness Navigator as a transferable business asset—not only a practice built around the founder's personal effort.

The design goal is a business that can:

- Use the founder's judgment, relationships, and personal strengths as the starting advantage.
- Convert those strengths into documented methods, standards, training, tools, and quality controls.
- Serve more clients through a carefully trained team.
- Operate consistently in multiple B.C. locations.
- Support company-owned branches, managed teams, licensed operators, or franchises.
- Continue operating during a founder absence.
- Produce reliable financial and operating evidence.
- Be understandable, transferable, and attractive to a future buyer.

The business does not become more valuable merely by producing documents. Value is created when the documented system is used repeatedly, produces consistent results, protects clients, earns sustainable profit, and can be operated by people other than the founder.

## Non-Negotiable Design Rules

1. The founder may be the original expert, but the company must own and maintain the business system.
2. Every recurring activity must eventually have an owner, procedure, standard, measurement, and training method.
3. Personal client information and medical information must not become part of sale or franchise materials unless there is a lawful, necessary, and professionally approved basis.
4. Client trust, consent, privacy, safety, and non-clinical boundaries cannot be weakened to make the model easier to scale.
5. A second operator must prove the system before the business is described as replicable.
6. A second location must prove the location model before broad expansion.
7. Franchising must not begin until the service, economics, brand, support system, disclosure obligations, and franchise relationship have received specialist review.
8. Financial reports must distinguish founder labour, unpaid work, one-time costs, recurring revenue, and location-level performance.
9. Important knowledge must be stored in business-controlled accounts, not only in the founder's memory, personal email, or personal devices.
10. Each quarter, the business must identify and reduce one meaningful founder dependency.

## Recommended Build Sequence

1. Prove that clients will pay for a well-defined service.
2. Standardize the service without removing necessary human judgment.
3. Measure time, cost, quality, and client experience.
4. Train one employee or contractor to perform defined portions safely.
5. Prove that the founder can step out of routine delivery.
6. Prove a team-led local operation.
7. Test a second service area or location under company control.
8. Compare company-owned, managed, licensed, partnership, and franchise expansion.
9. Build the chosen legal and support structure with qualified advisors.
10. Maintain buyer-ready evidence continuously rather than assembling it only when selling.

## Quarterly Owner Review

Ask and document:

- What still depends exclusively on the founder?
- Which client outcomes vary by worker or location?
- Which procedure was followed inconsistently?
- Which training or quality-control gap appeared?
- Is recurring revenue increasing?
- Is owner-adjusted profit improving?
- Are client concentration and referral concentration decreasing?
- Is the brand becoming more recognizable independently of the founder?
- Can the business recover from a 30-day founder absence?
- Are privacy, safety, legal, insurance, and franchise-readiness controls current?

## Professional Review Notice

The materials in this folder are strategic and operational planning tools. Corporate structure, tax, employment, privacy, intellectual property, securities, sale transactions, licensing, and franchising require advice from qualified professionals using the facts and documents of the actual business.
