# Fixer Profitability Model

## Purpose

This document translates the current Fixer business plan into an initial
profitability model for a founder-led, white-glove health and wellness navigation
service.

The figures are planning assumptions rather than forecasts. They should be
replaced with actual time, cost, conversion, and client-retention data from the
controlled pilots.

Unless otherwise stated, figures are in Canadian dollars, exclude income tax, and
do not treat collected GST as revenue.

## Executive Finding

At the current planning level of **$10,000 per month in revenue**, Fixer can be a
profitable founder-operated practice, but the margin is fragile. It becomes
unprofitable in economic terms if white-glove contact expands beyond defined
hours.

The current prices may be adequate for carefully bounded pilots. They are likely
too low for a mature, staffed white-glove service.

## Monthly Profitability Scenarios

These scenarios assume approximately $2,500–$3,500 per month for insurance,
software, professional support, bookkeeping, marketing, travel, payment costs,
and other operating expenses.

| Scenario | Revenue | Total founder hours | Non-founder expenses | Cash available to founder | Founder labour value | True operating profit |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Current plan, tightly managed | $10,000 | 120 | $2,500 | $7,500 | $6,000 | $1,500 / 15% |
| Current plan with scope creep | $10,000 | 170 | $3,000 | $7,000 | $8,500 | **–$1,500 / –15%** |
| Premium white-glove model | $15,000 | 120 | $3,500 | $11,500 | $6,000 | $5,500 / 37% |

“Cash available to founder” is not all profit. It includes payment for the
founder's client work, sales, administration, management, and risk.

The more meaningful measure is owner-adjusted operating profit after valuing
founder labour. A business is not truly profitable if it depends on substantial
unrecorded or unpaid founder work.

## Profitability of the Current Offers

The following model uses plausible all-in delivery time. Actual hours must be
measured during every pilot.

| Service | Current average price | Likely total hours | Revenue per delivery hour | Initial assessment |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Clarity Session | $225 | 2.5–3 | $75–$90 | Too low for staffed delivery |
| Two-Week Fix | $750 | 8–10 | $75–$94 | Vulnerable to scope creep |
| Ongoing Concierge | $1,000 | 8–10 | $100–$125 | Viable only with strict limits |
| Family Update | $225 | 1.5–2 | $113–$150 | Potentially sound if standardized |

Delivery hours must include:

- Fit-call allocation
- Intake and meeting time
- Research
- Provider contact
- Written deliverables
- Client communication
- Family communication
- Travel
- Record keeping
- Billing and closeout
- Follow-up
- Rework and failed contacts

If only the obvious client-facing time is counted, the packages will appear more
profitable than they really are.

## Offer-Level Analysis

### Clarity Session

At $225, a three-hour all-in engagement generates $75 per hour before software,
insurance, marketing, administration, and tax.

It can work as:

- A founder-delivered entry offer
- A lead-in to a larger package
- A tightly templated service
- A pilot used to learn what clients value

It is unlikely to support a qualified employee, supervision, administration, and
a healthy company margin at that price.

A mature white-glove version may need to be closer to **$350–$450**, subject to
market validation.

### Two-Week Fix

At $750, this offer becomes dangerous once communication and coordination exceed
approximately eight hours.

A potential mature structure is:

- $1,000–$1,400 base price
- Eight clearly included hours
- Defined update cadence
- One authorized family recipient
- Defined appointment and research limits
- Written approval for additional hours
- Overage rate of approximately $125–$150 per hour

Alternatively, the $750 pilot price could be retained while sharply narrowing the
promised outcome.

### Ongoing Concierge

This service should be priced as reserved coordination capacity, not merely as
hours used.

A high-touch concierge client creates obligations even during a quiet week:

- The business must remain familiar with the case.
- Availability must be protected.
- Records and permissions must stay current.
- The navigator must monitor open matters.
- Backup coverage must exist.
- Proactive updates must continue.

Potential mature tiers for testing are:

- **$1,500 per month:** Up to six or eight hours with defined contact standards
- **$2,000–$2,500 per month:** Higher-touch coordination with additional family
  communication
- Additional work billed separately with informed approval

Unused capacity should generally not accumulate indefinitely.

### Family Updates

This add-on can be profitable if it uses a controlled structure. Define:

- Number of recipients
- Frequency
- Format
- Length
- Whether a call is included
- How additional family communication is billed

Family dynamics can turn a $225 add-on into several hours of emotionally complex
work.

## Real Break-Even Point

### $90,000 Founder-Compensation Target

Assume:

- $30,000 annual non-founder operating expenses
- $90,000 annual founder compensation
- 15% target true operating margin

Required annual revenue:

```text
($30,000 + $90,000) ÷ 0.85 = $141,176
```

Required average monthly revenue: **approximately $11,765**.

### $100,000 Founder-Compensation Target

Assume the same operating expenses and margin with $100,000 in founder
compensation:

```text
($30,000 + $100,000) ÷ 0.85 = $152,941
```

Required average monthly revenue: **approximately $12,745**.

### Revenue Interpretation

- **$10,000 per month:** Viable founder income, but limited true profit
- **$12,000–$13,000 per month:** More credible sustainable founder practice
- **$15,000–$18,000 per month:** Room for stronger profit, support staff, and
  reinvestment
- **Above $20,000 per month:** Likely requires delegation and carefully managed
  capacity

## Recommended Target Economics

Use the following as provisional planning targets:

- Blended revenue per delivery hour: **at least $125**
- Founder-signature work: **$150 or more per delivery hour**
- Service contribution margin before general overhead: **55%–65%**
- Owner-adjusted operating margin: **15%–25%**
- Recurring revenue: **50%–65% of total revenue**
- No unlimited communication or 24/7 access
- Included hours and overage rules for every package
- All founder hours recorded, including sales and administration
- No package treated as proven until at least 10–20 examples have been measured

These are management hypotheses. Pilot evidence should determine the final
targets and prices.

## Hiring Implications

The current model can support a small amount of part-time administrative help,
but probably not a substantial team at $10,000 per month.

The Government of Canada Job Bank reports a B.C. median wage of approximately
$28.50 per hour for administrative assistants. At 20 hours per week, that is about
$29,640 annually in wages before employer costs, paid leave, equipment, and
management time.

A part-time coordinator will likely require approximately **$50,000–$60,000 of
additional annual revenue** to cover the employment cost and preserve margin. The
hire makes sense when it releases enough founder time to generate more than that
amount through higher-value work.

Government wage references also indicate approximate medians of $26 per hour for
a social and community-service worker in the Thompson–Okanagan and $36.81 per
hour for a social worker in B.C. These provide general reference points for future
navigator roles, although Fixer's exact position, responsibilities, and required
qualifications may differ.

## GST and Tax Consideration

At $10,000 per month, the business would exceed the Canada Revenue Agency's
$30,000 small-supplier threshold quickly if the services are taxable supplies. An
accountant should confirm the tax treatment of the actual services and whether
public prices will be quoted before or including GST.

Collected GST is not revenue and should not be treated as available operating
cash.

Source:

- [Canada Revenue Agency — Liability for tax and the small-supplier threshold](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/forms-publications/publications/3-1/liability-tax.html)

## Most Profitable Early Business Shape

The strongest early model is likely:

- A founder-led premium practice
- Five to eight recurring concierge clients
- A limited number of higher-priced Two-Week Fixes
- Clarity Sessions used selectively as an entry point
- Standardized family updates and educational products
- A part-time coordinator handling scheduling, reminders, records, and directory
  work
- Founder time concentrated on intake, judgment, relationships, complex planning,
  and quality review

The business should avoid trying to make money through a high volume of low-priced
Clarity Sessions. Its economic advantage lies in trusted recurring relationships,
premium coordination, carefully limited capacity, and delegation of lower-value
administrative work.

## Pilot Measurement Requirements

For every pilot engagement, record:

- Package and price
- All founder hours
- All non-founder hours
- Research and provider-contact time
- Client and family communication time
- Travel time and cost
- Direct third-party costs absorbed by the business
- Rework and failed-contact time
- Amount billed as overage
- Client acquisition source and acquisition time
- Contribution before general overhead
- Effective revenue per delivery hour
- Whether the client renewed or referred another client

Review the results after every pilot and formally reconsider prices after the
first five, 10, and 20 completed engagements.

## Final Profitability Principle

> Fixer must price for the entire relationship burden—not just the visible task.

White-glove service should reduce the client's coordination burden without
creating uncontrolled, unpaid work for the business.
