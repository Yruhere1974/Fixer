# Fixer Premium White-Glove Pricing Model

**Version:** 2.0 planning model

**Date:** July 16, 2026

**Status:** Management hypothesis for controlled pilots—not a forecast or approved
public price list

## Purpose

This model replaces task-based introductory pricing with a premium, relationship-
based structure for clients who value discretion, continuity, proactive service,
and relief from complex health and wellness logistics.

Fixer remains a non-clinical navigation and coordination service. It does not sell
medical access, diagnosis, treatment, emergency response, or health outcomes.

All figures are Canadian dollars, exclude income tax and GST, and must be tested
against actual client demand, delivery time, labour cost, insurance, legal advice,
and tax treatment.

## Executive Decision

The original prices—$175–$250 for a Clarity Session, $600–$900 for a Two-Week
Fix, and $750–$1,500 per month for ongoing service—do not safely fund a true
white-glove experience. They leave too little room for proactive contact, backup
coverage, family dynamics, failed provider contacts, documentation, service
recovery, and founder oversight.

The recommended model has four commercial rules:

1. Begin every substantive relationship with a paid Private Needs Assessment.
2. Sell ongoing service as reserved capacity through a monthly retainer.
3. Price every package to remain profitable when all included capacity is used.
4. Stop, obtain approval, or upgrade the package before additional work begins.

The recommended initial prices are:

| Offer | Price | Commercial role |
|---|---:|---|
| Private Needs Assessment | $750 once | Paid assessment and coordination roadmap |
| Essential Private Coordination | $2,500/month | One client, controlled complexity |
| Signature Family Coordination | $4,500/month | Higher-touch client or family coordination |
| Private Office | From $7,500/month | Complex household or multi-party mandate |

The model deliberately targets a smaller number of higher-value relationships.
It should not be diluted with unlimited communication or deeply discounted custom
work.

## What “White Glove” Includes

White-glove service means:

- One named relationship lead and a named backup
- A documented client preference and communication profile
- Proactive updates, including when there is no external progress
- Curated options with verification appropriate to the request
- Warm handoffs that do not require the client to repeat their story
- Appointment and logistics preparation
- Clear written next steps and promise tracking
- Discreet, consent-based family communication
- Service recovery owned by Fixer when something goes wrong

It does **not** mean:

- Unlimited hours, messages, meetings, research, or family recipients
- 24/7 availability or emergency/crisis response
- Diagnosis, clinical advice, treatment, or interpretation of symptoms
- Guaranteed appointments, access, recovery, or provider outcomes
- Absorbing third-party expenses or extensive travel
- Immediate work outside the agreed service window

## Pricing Architecture

### 1. Private Needs Assessment — $750

A required starting engagement for new clients unless the founder approves a
different paid scoping process.

Includes:

- Up to 90 minutes of structured intake
- Review of information supplied by the client
- Defined objectives, risks, constraints, permissions, and budget
- A written 30-day coordination roadmap
- A recommended service tier and initial priorities
- One 30-minute roadmap review
- Up to four all-in delivery hours

Boundary: this assessment produces a plan; it does not include open-ended booking,
provider chasing, appointment attendance, or ongoing family communication.

Target economics at four hours:

| Measure | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Revenue | $750 |
| Maximum direct-delivery cost budget (30%) | $225 |
| Variable-cost reserve (5%) | $38 |
| Target contribution before fixed overhead | $487 / 65% |

The assessment fee may be credited by up to $250 against the first retainer only
when the client signs a three-month engagement within seven days. Do not credit
the full fee: the assessment is a real deliverable and consumes capacity.

### 2. Essential Private Coordination — $2,500 per month

Designed for one client with a defined mandate and limited stakeholder complexity.

Includes:

- One named relationship lead and backup
- Up to 10 all-in delivery hours per month
- One active coordination roadmap
- One scheduled proactive update each week
- Business-hours acknowledgement within one business day
- Research and coordination for up to two active priorities at a time
- One authorized family recipient
- One scheduled 30-minute client/family call per month
- Appointment preparation and written next steps

Minimum initial term: three months. Thereafter, month-to-month with 30 days'
notice, subject to contract review.

### 3. Signature Family Coordination — $4,500 per month

Designed for a higher-touch client or family with more moving parts, while still
maintaining a defined scope.

Includes:

- Up to 20 all-in delivery hours per month
- Up to four active priorities at a time
- Up to three authorized family recipients
- Weekly written update plus up to two scheduled calls per month
- Business-hours acknowledgement within four business hours
- Provider research, scheduling, warm handoffs, logistics preparation, and
  follow-through within the agreed mandate
- Up to one local appointment attendance or in-person coordination visit per
  month; travel time counts toward included capacity
- Quarterly service-plan review for engagements continuing beyond three months

Minimum initial term: three months. This is the recommended flagship offer.

### 4. Private Office — from $7,500 per month

Designed for a complex household, geographically distant family, or mandate with
multiple stakeholders and a need for more deeply managed logistics.

Starting scope includes:

- Up to 35 all-in delivery hours per month
- A lead navigator, named backup, and founder oversight
- Up to six active priorities at a time
- Up to five authorized family or professional contacts
- A tailored proactive communication cadence
- Up to two local appointment attendances or in-person visits per month; travel
  time counts toward included capacity
- Monthly private coordination review and forward plan
- Priority scheduling within published business hours

This tier requires a written scope and capacity review. Mandates involving
frequent travel, multiple clients, substantial document reconstruction, unusual
privacy/security requirements, or extensive after-hours coordination are quoted
above $7,500.

## Additional Work and Pass-Through Costs

Additional work starts only after documented approval:

| Work type | Rate |
|---|---:|
| Coordinator/administrative execution | $150/hour |
| Navigator coordination | $225/hour |
| Founder-led complex planning, advocacy preparation, or review | $325/hour |
| Local travel time | Applicable team-member rate |
| Vehicle distance | Current CRA reasonable per-kilometre allowance or actual approved transport cost |
| Rush request accepted inside one business day | 1.5× applicable rate |
| Evening/weekend work accepted in advance | 1.5× applicable rate |

- Bill in 15-minute increments after a minimum 30-minute charge for unscheduled
  work.
- Third-party costs require approval and are prepaid or charged to the client's
  own account wherever possible.
- Do not mark up regulated health services or accept referral commissions.
- A deposit may be required for travel, large purchases, or third-party bookings.
- Unused included hours do not roll over. The retainer also pays for continuity,
  familiarity, proactive monitoring, and reserved capacity.

## Unit-Economics Guardrails

Every tier must satisfy these targets at **100% use of included hours**:

| Guardrail | Minimum target |
|---|---:|
| Package contribution before fixed overhead | 60%; plan at 65% |
| Direct-delivery labour | No more than 30% of revenue |
| Payment and other variable-cost reserve | 5% of revenue |
| Owner-adjusted operating margin | 20% minimum; target 25%–30% |
| Blended package revenue per included hour | At least $200 |
| Founder-signature work | At least $325/hour when billed separately |
| Recurring revenue | 70%–85% of total revenue |
| Largest client | Below 25% of trailing three-month revenue |
| Largest referral source | Below 30% of trailing 12-month new revenue |

### Full-Use Stress Test

The table below uses a direct-delivery cost ceiling equal to 30% of price. This
cost envelope includes wages or founder replacement cost, payroll burden,
supervision attributable to delivery, and delivery-related administration.

| Tier | Price | Included hours | Revenue/included hour | Labour ceiling | Variable reserve | Contribution | Contribution % |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Essential | $2,500 | 10 | $250 | $750 | $125 | $1,625 | 65% |
| Signature | $4,500 | 20 | $225 | $1,350 | $225 | $2,925 | 65% |
| Private Office | $7,500 | 35 | $214 | $2,250 | $375 | $4,875 | 65% |

If actual delivery labour exceeds the ceiling for two consecutive months, the
business must re-scope, raise the price, move the client to a higher tier, or end
the engagement safely. Founder time is never treated as free.

## Capacity and Staffing Model

Do not sell every nominal delivery hour. White-glove quality requires room for
delays, urgent-but-non-emergency issues, quality review, absence coverage, and
service recovery.

- Schedule no more than 75% of practical team delivery capacity as included
  retainer hours.
- Reserve 15% for variability, recovery, and accepted rush work.
- Reserve 10% for training, documentation, quality review, and continuity.
- Count fit calls, notes, provider follow-up, family communication, travel,
  billing clarification, rework, and closeout as delivery time.
- Limit the founder to work requiring founder judgment, relationship depth, sales,
  complex review, or service recovery.

The theoretical number of clients is less important than the measured capacity
load. Enrollment closes when the next client would push committed included hours
above 75% of practical capacity.

## Illustrative Monthly Portfolio

An initial premium practice could target three Essential clients and three
Signature clients, plus one new assessment per month.

| Item | Monthly amount |
|---|---:|
| Recurring retainer revenue | $21,000 |
| One Private Needs Assessment | $750 |
| Total revenue | $21,750 |
| Maximum direct-delivery labour at 30% | ($6,525) |
| Variable-cost reserve at 5% | ($1,088) |
| Fixed operating overhead assumption | ($4,000) |
| Founder management/sales replacement-cost assumption | ($3,000) |
| Owner-adjusted operating profit | **$7,137 / 32.8%** |

This is a capacity and pricing illustration, not a forecast. It assumes the
founder's direct client-delivery time is already included in direct labour and
does not count the same time twice.

### Downside Test

At the same $21,750 revenue, if direct labour rises to 40% and fixed overhead plus
founder management reaches $8,000:

```text
$21,750 revenue
– $8,700 direct labour
– $1,088 variable-cost reserve
– $8,000 fixed overhead and founder management
= $3,962 owner-adjusted operating profit (18.2%)
```

That result falls below the 20% floor and triggers corrective action. The model
therefore exposes scope creep before cash income is mistaken for profit.

## Scope-Control and Risk Rules

### Capacity warnings

- At 70% of included hours used: send an internal warning and review remaining
  priorities with the client.
- At 85%: obtain the client's decision to defer, approve additional work, or
  upgrade.
- At 100%: pause non-urgent work until written approval or the next billing period.
- Safety concerns are handled under the escalation protocol, not ignored because
  a package is exhausted.

### Automatic re-scope triggers

Review the tier and price when any of the following occurs:

- Included capacity is exceeded twice in a rolling three-month period.
- Active priorities or authorized contacts repeatedly exceed the package.
- More than 20% of delivery time is spent on unplanned family communication.
- Travel, appointment attendance, rework, or failed contacts become routine.
- The requested response cadence requires de facto on-call coverage.
- The mandate changes materially or a second client is added.
- The direct-labour ceiling or contribution floor is breached.

### Payment protection

- Retainers are paid in advance by automatic payment method.
- Assessments and deposits are paid before work begins.
- A payment method is retained for approved overages and pass-through costs.
- Work may pause for overdue balances, subject to safe handoff and contractual
  obligations.
- Prices are reviewed at renewal and at least annually.
- Agreements include cancellation, refund, suspension, chargeback evidence, and
  third-party-cost rules reviewed by B.C. legal counsel.

## Client Positioning

Lead with relief, discretion, and continuity—not hours:

> A private coordination service for individuals and families who want one trusted
> point of contact to organize health and wellness logistics, keep everyone aligned,
> and carry details through to completion.

The client buys reduced effort and greater confidence. The internal capacity
limits protect the quality of that promise; they should be clear in the agreement
without turning the public message into an hourly-rate sheet.

“Private Office” and “concierge” must never imply private medical treatment,
preferential access to insured health services, or clinical expertise. Marketing
and contracts should consistently say **non-clinical navigation and coordination**.

## Pilot and Price-Validation Plan

Do not publish all tiers as permanent until they have been tested.

### Recommended launch sequence

1. Sell five paid Private Needs Assessments at $750.
2. Convert suitable clients into no more than three Essential and three Signature
   pilot relationships at the full proposed price.
3. Offer Private Office only by founder-approved proposal during the pilot.
4. Review economics after five assessments and after each retainer's first,
   second, and third month.
5. Increase, narrow, or retire any offer that fails the guardrails.

For every engagement, record:

- Price, discounts, credits, refunds, and payment fees
- All founder and non-founder time by activity
- Direct labour at actual cost and replacement cost
- Active priorities and authorized contacts
- Communication, family, travel, provider-contact, and rework time
- Capacity warnings and overage approvals
- Direct third-party costs absorbed by Fixer
- Contribution before fixed overhead
- Owner-adjusted operating profit
- Client acquisition source, cost, and founder sales time
- Renewal, referral, client effort, confidence, and service-recovery outcomes

No package is proven until at least 10–20 representative engagements or client-
months have been measured. A premium price should be validated by willingness to
pay, retention, referrals, low client effort, and healthy contribution—not by
founder confidence alone.

## Tax, Legal, and Ethical Review Gates

- At these prices, the business can exceed the GST/HST small-supplier threshold
  quickly if its services are taxable. Confirm registration timing, tax status,
  invoicing, and whether prices are displayed before or including GST with an
  accountant.
- Have B.C. counsel review the service description, retainer structure, renewal,
  cancellation, payment, privacy, consent, liability, and safe-handoff terms.
- Confirm insurance covers the precise white-glove activities, appointment
  attendance, transport/logistics role, family communications, and staff model.
- Keep third-party recommendations independent and disclose conflicts. Do not let
  price or client status change safety, privacy, consent, or provider-verification
  standards.
- Preserve a documented route to reduced-fee work, pro bono capacity, or community
  referral if desired; do not undermine the commercial model through ad hoc
  discounting.

## Management Approval Checklist

Before these prices become public, approve:

- Exact deliverables and exclusions for each tier
- Practical team capacity and loaded replacement cost by role
- Response windows, business hours, travel radius, and rush-work rules
- Who may authorize discounts, credits, overages, and service recovery
- Contract, privacy, tax, insurance, and marketing-language review
- The measurement dashboard and monthly contribution review
- The maximum number of clients accepted at each staffing level

## Final Pricing Principle

> Fixer prices the responsibility, continuity, reserved capacity, and entire
> relationship burden—not merely the visible task.

The safest premium model is not the one with the highest headline price. It is the
one that makes the promise explicit, limits the burden, measures every hour, and
remains economically sound when the client uses everything included.
