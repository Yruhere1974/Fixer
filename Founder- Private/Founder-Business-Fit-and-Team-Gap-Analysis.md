# Founder–Business Fit and Team Gap Analysis

## Purpose

This review compares Marcia Wilson's background and lived experience with the
proposed Fixer health and wellness navigation business. It identifies strong
founder–business crossover, likely capability gaps, and areas where professional
support or future team capacity will be needed.

This document contains personal information derived from the founder profile and
résumé. Keep it in the private `Founder` workspace rather than publishing it in a
public repository.

## Overall Assessment

Marcia has unusually strong founder–business fit for Fixer's first stage. Her
experience directly matches the core service: helping overwhelmed clients clarify
goals, navigate systems, coordinate people, identify barriers, and move toward
action.

The main weakness is not service delivery. It is building the regulated,
commercially disciplined, privacy-safe organization around that service—and
ensuring the company does not become completely dependent on Marcia's judgment,
energy, and relationships.

## Where the Crossover Is Strongest

### 1. Client Navigation and Action Planning

Marcia's occupational therapy background closely matches the proposed client
journey: structured intake, identifying barriers, coordinating stakeholders,
creating practical plans, and supporting client decisions. Her clinical experience
across long-term care, neurology, acute care, community care, and chronic illness
supports this conclusion.

This is almost a direct match for Fixer's intake, action-map, research,
coordination, and follow-through services.

Best founder responsibilities:

- Conduct early fit calls and complex intakes.
- Design the action-map method.
- Handle ambiguous or emotionally difficult cases.
- Teach future navigators how to identify barriers and priorities.

### 2. Aging-Parent and Complex-Care Niche

The initial niche—busy adults coordinating care for themselves or an aging
parent—is particularly credible for Marcia. She has professional experience with
long-term care, discharge planning, chronic disease, caregivers, rehabilitation,
and community services, as well as lived family-care experience.

This niche is a stronger starting point than broad “wellness.” It aligns with the
business plan's recommended first market and the founder profile's aging-parent
and complex-health categories.

### 3. Advocacy and Stakeholder Coordination

Marcia's work connecting Interior Health, physicians, the MS Society, government,
and community organizations is excellent preparation for building referral
relationships. Her MS Clinic advocacy and Division of Family Practice work show
that she can operate between institutions rather than only inside one profession.

That experience translates well into:

- Referral-partner development
- Provider conversations
- Family meetings
- Difficult follow-up
- Educational presentations
- Community partnership development

### 4. Trust-Building and Communication

Fixer sells relief, clarity, confidence, and reliable follow-through. Marcia's
combination of empathy, teaching, public speaking, writing, coaching, and client
support is well suited to that promise.

Her best marketing advantage will probably be educational authority rather than
aggressive promotion, including:

- Caregiver workshops
- “How to prepare for discharge” guides
- Aging-parent organization resources
- Appointment-preparation tools
- Referral-partner presentations

### 5. Entrepreneurship and Operational Experience

Marcia has already built and sold a service business, managed staff and
contractors, served thousands of customers, handled partnerships, and operated
programs. This substantially reduces the typical first-time-founder risk.

Her experience also supports Fixer's longer-term objective: turning personal
expertise into a repeatable organization rather than remaining a solo practice.

### 6. Team Leadership and Knowledge Transfer

Marcia has hired, mentored, trained, supervised, and structured roles around
people's strengths. That aligns well with the proposed transition from founder-led
delivery to coordinators and navigators.

The key challenge will be converting intuitive judgment into observable rules,
examples, escalation triggers, and quality standards.

## Where Support Will Be Needed

### 1. Legal Scope, Professional Identity, and Insurance — Highest Priority

Marcia's clinical credibility is an asset, but it also creates scope risk. Clients
may interpret her occupational therapy background as permission to assess
symptoms, recommend treatment, or provide clinical guidance, even when Fixer is
intended to be non-clinical.

Qualified help is needed to determine:

- How her OT history and any current registration may be presented
- Whether she will operate entirely outside OT practice
- Which professional and commercial policies apply
- What insurance descriptions and exclusions are appropriate
- How contracts, disclaimers, consent, and emergency procedures should work

Required support:

- B.C. health or business lawyer
- Insurance broker familiar with health-adjacent services

A disclaimer alone is insufficient. The actual service behaviour, documentation,
marketing, and client expectations must all remain aligned.

### 2. Privacy, Security, and Sensitive-Information Governance

Clinical confidentiality experience is valuable, but operating a private company
under its own privacy obligations is a different competency. Fixer needs consent
management, authorized-recipient controls, retention and destruction, breach
response, vendor review, secure communications, role-based access, and incident
documentation.

Required support:

- B.C. privacy professional or lawyer
- Security-conscious technology consultant
- Periodic independent privacy review

Marcia should develop enough privacy literacy to supervise the system, but she
should not be the only person certifying that it is adequate.

### 3. Commercial Validation and Disciplined Selling

Marcia has fundraising, direct-sales, marketing, and customer-development
experience. What is not yet demonstrated is repeatable acquisition for a
high-trust, private-pay navigation service at the proposed prices.

The risk is assuming that strong community interest equals willingness to pay.

Development priorities:

- Short, structured discovery interviews
- Consultative selling without over-helping for free
- Clear qualification and fit decisions
- Consistent pricing and scope control
- Conversion tracking by referral source
- Comfort asking for payment in advance

Do not hire a salesperson initially. Founder-led selling will reveal what clients
actually buy. A marketing specialist becomes useful only after the first offer
converts reliably.

### 4. Unit Economics and Financial Controls

Marcia's résumé shows meaningful budget and cash-flow experience. The new
challenge is determining whether each package remains profitable after counting
all research, communication, travel, administration, follow-up, supervision, and
rework.

The business's $10,000 monthly scenario is illustrative, and coordination work can
expand beyond its price unless scope and communication limits are explicit.

Required support:

- Accountant for structure, tax, GST, and worker classification
- Bookkeeper for a clean monthly close
- Possibly a fractional CFO later, but not at launch

Marcia should personally learn to read the package-level scorecard. All founder
time should be recorded at a reasonable replacement cost.

### 5. Technology Architecture and Administration

Marcia is comfortable troubleshooting small websites, but the source documents do
not demonstrate experience designing secure systems for sensitive client
information.

Required support:

- Independent technology and privacy advisor for software selection
- Managed IT support for accounts, devices, backups, access, and offboarding
- Accessible web designer once the offer is settled

Do not hire a CTO or build custom software early. Start with carefully selected
existing tools and fictional-client testing.

### 6. Current Kelowna Network Depth and Verification

Marcia has strong healthcare and community relationships, but much of the
documented entrepreneurial and institutional experience is based in Kamloops. Her
recent Interior Health work helps, but a current, verified Kelowna network still
has to be built.

This requires more than networking. The directory needs credential checks,
pricing, accessibility information, wait times, cultural considerations, privacy
practices, and recurring re-verification.

Marcia should initially define the relationship strategy. Routine maintenance can
later transfer to a provider-network coordinator.

### 7. Personal Capacity and Continuity

Marcia's lived experience makes her credible, but chronic health challenges,
concussion recovery, and family responsibilities may also make an emotionally
intensive concierge model harder to deliver consistently.

This is a design consideration rather than a reason not to proceed. The business
should include:

- Published working and response hours
- No 24/7 promise
- Strict package boundaries
- Caseload limits
- Backup coverage
- Standard communication windows
- Early delegation of scheduling and document handling
- A wait-list trigger before quality deteriorates

The business will attract clients who are overwhelmed. Without boundaries, their
urgency can become the founder's urgency.

### 8. Founder Dependence

The founder profile says people will buy because “Marcia will help us figure this
out.” That is powerful for launch and dangerous for scale.

Keep as founder signature work:

- Vision and positioning
- Early sales and fit decisions
- High-trust referral relationships
- Complex action planning
- High-risk escalations
- Training and quality standards
- Public education and thought leadership

Delegate first:

- Scheduling and reminders
- Document assembly
- Directory maintenance
- Routine follow-up
- Invoice preparation
- Status tracking
- Approved family updates

The first operational hire should probably be a part-time client coordinator, not
another senior navigator.

## Recommended Support Structure

### Before Paid Clients

- B.C. lawyer or privacy professional
- Insurance broker
- Accountant and bookkeeper
- Technology and security advisor
- Clinical or safeguarding advisor for escalation protocols

### After the First Few Pilots

- Part-time client coordinator
- Contract provider-directory support
- Web and brand implementation support
- Quarterly advisory circle

### After Approximately 20 Proven Engagements

- Train a second navigator.
- Establish independent quality review.
- Transfer selected engagements under supervision.
- Test progressively longer founder absences.

## What Marcia Should Develop Personally

The highest-value founder-development areas are:

- Scope discipline: knowing when not to help
- Consultative sales and price conversations
- Package-level time and margin measurement
- Written delegation and quality criteria
- Privacy oversight
- Capturing intuitive judgment as teachable methods
- Making decisions from pilot evidence rather than possibility

Marcia does not need to become the lawyer, privacy specialist, accountant,
cybersecurity expert, or software developer.

## Conclusion

Marcia is very well suited to inventing and proving the Fixer service. The early
company should be built around her navigation judgment, credibility, and
relationships, with specialist risk support in place immediately and
administrative capacity added relatively early.

The first team should protect founder time and constrain risk. Only later should
the company attempt to replicate the founder's highest-judgment work.
